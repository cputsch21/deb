## The opening screen

You open MyOS. You're standing in **the Void** — the calm floor of the whole app. Behind everything: the quote, rotating, context-aware (it knows it's 6:40 AM and that this was a heavy week). Tap it and it freezes — riding into the mentor's context as today's ambient mood. Near the date, faint in the margin: sunrise 6:12 · sunset 8:39, visible on hover or when the moment is near.

Front and center: **the conversation** — your one thread with the mentor, exactly where you left it. The composer sits at the bottom: one input, one "+" that tucks everything else (attach, voice, web search). One door.

Along the edge (desktop: a thin left rail; mobile: a compact picker): **the lenses** — the Mentor at top, then your projects: CTDI · Subseven · ISO · 150 Poplar · Family · Personal. Each is a small mark in its project's color. No labels shouting, no counts nagging. **[OPEN-1: rail always visible on desktop, or revealed on hover/keystroke?]**

Above the composer, only when it has something to say: **Now** — the one glance surface. A short strip of ranked chips: what's due, what's slipping, what you owe someone, where you left off last night. Each chip wears its project's color on its edge and carries a ≤12-word why. It's ephemeral: gone when empty, collapses the moment you start typing, blooms back when you're at rest. Never a score, never a streak. **[OPEN-2: strip above the composer (TRUE's answer) vs a top-of-screen ribbon? I lean above-composer — it's where your eyes already are.]**

That's the whole resting state: void + quote + conversation + lenses + (sometimes) Now. Nothing else exists until summoned.

## Entering a project

Tap ISO. The whole app repaints — ISO's world: its own muted scheme over both light and dark, the same calm geometry in a different material. Same mind, narrowed focus: the thread is now the ISO conversation, Now shows only ISO's reality, the project's derived status card is one ask away ("where are we?"). The Mentor's own world (the whole-life lens) keeps a reserved scheme no project can take — coming home feels like coming home.

Arc lives here too: **Arc mode is the Mentor's world done as ambience** — the deep, atmospheric treatment you loved, reserved for the whole-life lens, so "home" has a soul the project rooms don't compete with. *(We'll design this properly in the visual phase.)*

## When things arrive

Your Plaud call syncs; three reMarkable pages land; you paste a wall of notes into the chat. The AI reads everything against the citation bar. Confident stuff just files — a quiet receipt chip in the thread ("Filed — Call · Larry · 38m"), nothing else. Anything ambiguous gets one inline line in chat: "Context, or do you want to talk about it?"

And here the SwipeWrite DNA comes back as a first-class surface: **the Deck** — the decision engine. The AI harvests *proposed actions* from everything that flows in — meeting transcripts, reMarkable pages, emails, things you said in chat — plus system-born cards: a filing that needs a project call, a bench task gone stale ("still want this?"), a recurring beat that broke its streak. Each is one card: the action verbatim, its provenance ("from yesterday's call with Larry, 3:02"), the project guess, the On-You context.

When cards accumulate, a soft badge appears on the rail, echoed by one quiet line in Now ("6 to decide"). Tap it and you're in: full-screen, one card at a time, rapid fire. **Each swipe is a decision, never an execution** — the point isn't to do the thing, it's to decide what happens to the thing. The grammar is **the four D's** (Chris's ruling, July 22):

- **→ Do** — it's mine and it matters now; lands in Now/today.
- **↑ Delay** — quick date (defaults tomorrow); it sleeps until its day.
- **← Delegate** — pick the person; it becomes a tracked Waiting-On with a chase date. The swipe where things you shouldn't do yourself get handed off.
- **↓ Delete** — not a thing; teaches the extractor (the cleared-task learning loop).

The directions deliberately mirror SwipeWrite's muscle memory: right = positive, up = later, down = junk, left = involves-another-person. Hold or tap a card for the finer moves (exact date, redirect project, edit wording). Swipes on mobile; the same grammar as arrow keys on desktop. Ten cards, twenty seconds, every open loop has a decision attached.

**Intake (ruling): everything, to start** — new AI-derived actions, uncertain filings, stale bench tasks, broken rhythms, and an on-demand "deal me my open loops" — then pare down by feel once it's lived in. Triage is a mode you enter and leave, never a place that looms.

## Saying things

"I need to send Larry an invoice." The task exists — instantly, no card, no confirm. The receipt is one calm line in the thread ("● Invoice Larry — added to CTDI"), and the line itself is tappable to edit or undo. A burst of actions coalesces into one Signed line, never a wall of chips. The only things that ever wait for your explicit yes: anything leaving the app, and the two permanent verdicts (done-forever, dropped-forever).

## The day's rhythm (all opt-in, zero ceremony)

Morning: you show up, Now is simply *right* — because last night's reMarkable page (or a one-line sign-off in chat) closed the day, and tomorrow was seeded from where you actually stopped. Asking "what should I do right now?" always gets one decisive answer, never a menu.

Friday afternoon, in the same thread, the mentor opens the impact check — what moved on each of the six worlds, what drifted, receipts attached. Ten minutes, conversational, done.

Any time: "show me my blindspots" / "how's my attention split this month?" — the invisible record surfaces on demand in the chat, then gets out of the way.

## Mobile = desktop, translated not shrunk

Equal citizens means the same five elements, re-expressed: the rail becomes a thumb-reach lens picker; the stack becomes true swipes; the composer's "+" carries voice capture front and center **[OPEN-4: the S11 voice-note strategy — a home-screen shortcut straight into capture? App-open-to-mic? This one deserves its own small design moment]**; Now sits in the same place above the composer. Nothing exists on one platform that can't be reached on the other.

## What deliberately does NOT exist

No dashboard. No standing inbox. No workspace/board. No Today panel. No settings sprawl (one sheet). No notification of any kind in V1. No score anywhere. The graveyard rules (L1–L7) are structural law: ceremony and chrome stay dead.
