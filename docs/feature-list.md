# MyOS — Feature List v1.1 (LOCKED)
*Distilled July 22, 2026 from Chris's rulings on all 166 inventory items (master + Kairos sheets), then locked with his answers to the five open questions. Small on purpose: everything here earned its place; everything cut is recorded in the sheets.*

## The one-sentence product

**One AI mentor, one conversation, one door for everything in my life — it quietly keeps my projects, goals, tasks, and promises current so that when I ask "what matters right now," the answer is right — and I get more done and feel more alive.**

Two theses, merged by your rulings: TRUE's *impact* (goals → reality) powered by Kairos's *presence* (stress less, feel more alive). The mentor persona is TRUE's — truth over comfort, peer not servant, receipts, restraint — warmed with a little humor.

---

## 1 · The Mentor

- **S1.** As a user, I have one continuous conversation with a named AI mentor (name hardcoded by us) who knows my whole life and everything I've ever told it. One thread, forever.
- **S2.** As a user, I can scope the conversation to a project — same mind, narrowed focus — and switch lenses instantly.
- **S3.** As a user, when I say something actionable ("I need to send Larry an invoice"), **the thing just gets created — no card, no confirm tap.** Everything is one tap to edit or delete afterward, so the AI acts and I correct, instead of the AI asking and me approving. *(Replaces the Handshake for internal writes; anything leaving the app — an email, a message — is still staged for my explicit send.)*
- **S4.** As a user, the mentor tells me the truth with dated receipts, labels facts vs judgment vs opinion, names my avoidance, and occasionally tells me NOT to work on something. I can overrule; it helps fully, no sulking.
- **S5.** As a user, the mentor knows when to stay silent — it doesn't reply to every message, and typing dots only appear once it has decided to speak.
- **S6.** As a user, I set how forward the mentor is with one dial (quiet butler → engaged partner).
- **S7.** As a user, the mentor coaches on my values: it reads my principles/ethos and my identity, and reflects them back with receipts ("every kept promise is a vote for that identity" — sparingly).
- **S8.** As a user, the mentor learns from being wrong: corrections, cleared tasks, and my feedback all feed **one** visible learning loop — lessons I can read, edit, and kill.

## 2 · One door (capture)

- **S9.** As a user, there is exactly **one place things go in: the chat box.** A thought, a file, a transcript, a link, a pasted wall of text — the AI recognizes what it is. If it's clearly context, it quietly files it and drops a receipt chip; if it's ambiguous, it asks in one line, right in the chat ("File this for the record, or do you want to talk about it?").
- **S10.** As a user, my Plaud recordings and reMarkable notes arrive automatically (via the private ingest address) — I record a call or write a page, and it shows up already understood.
- **S11.** As a user, I can capture a voice note from my phone in one tap. *(Concept approved; the mechanism needs a fresh strategy — UX phase.)*
- **S12.** As a user, everything filed is distilled against one bar — "would this be worth citing in an honest conversation about my life?" — and promises (mine and others') are extracted verbatim, because promises are gold.
- **S13.** As a user, filing never interrupts me, never nags, never auto-creates noise. Quiet receipts only.

## 3 · Triage & the Inbox

- **S14.** As a user, everything captured gets routed to the right project. Confident calls just happen (and the routing gets smarter every time I correct it); uncertain ones become a small stack I can blast through in seconds. *(The swipe-grade triage feel from SwipeWrite, reimagined — UX phase.)*
- **S15.** As a user, every conversation-shaped thing that comes in carries an **"On You"** verdict: nothing / needs a reply / the concrete thing I owe, verbatim ("send the signed contract by Fri").
- **S16.** As a user, I can set a **watch** in plain English ("tell me the moment the ISO auditor replies") and the moment it arrives, it's pinned in front of me.
- **S17.** As a user, important things are ranked with a visible "why it ranks here," and the sense of *important* is learned from how I actually behave — same single learning loop as S8.

## 4 · Structure (projects · goals · tasks)

- **S18.** As a user, I create projects freely (CTDI, Subseven, ISO, 150 Poplar, Family, Personal…). Every goal is a finishable outcome — done with a clean yes/no. I own every edit: titles, dates, status. The AI never owns my structure; it owns the argument about it.
- **S19.** As a user, a new project starts with a short intake conversation that ends in a one-line mission; the mentor judges (never stores) whether work aligns with it.
- **S20.** As a user, loose tasks are allowed — they sit on the Bench with dignity, get clustered into proposed goals when a theme emerges, and fade honestly instead of rotting into guilt.
- **S21.** As a user, I have recurring tasks (named exactly that) which materialize as normal tasks; a missed one is noise, a missed streak gets a "fix the rhythm or retire it" conversation, never a nag.
- **S22.** As a user, every project shows an always-current, derived status card — computed from the truth, never maintained by hand.
- **S23.** As a user, wishful planning gets named in conversation ("that goal has no real task under it") — it lives in the AI's judgment, not as a rule in the UI.

## 5 · Today (ONE daily surface — the D1/D2/D6 merger)

- **S24.** As a user, I get **one** answer to "what should I do right now?" — decisive, one thing, never a menu. Asking in chat always works.
- **S25.** As a user, there is **one** light glance surface for the day (working name: **Now**) — a short ranked strip blending my calendar-free reality: what's due, what's slipping, what I owe people, and where I left off yesterday. Each item carries a ≤12-word *why*. It is ephemeral — gone when empty, collapses when I engage, never scored, no streaks, no locks, no "won" days. *(Exact shape is THE central UX conversation.)*
- **S26.** As a user, I can **close the day** — tonight's reMarkable note (or a one-line sign-off in chat) is ingested, and tomorrow's Now starts from where I actually left off, not from a cold algorithm. *(This is the fix for why every previous "today list" flopped.)*
- **S27.** As a user, reminders resurface things at the right time — the item simply reappears; no buzz, no notification.

## 6 · People & promises

- **S28.** As a user, I see what I owe people and what people owe me — extracted from my calls and notes with owner and chase-by date — and the mentor raises the overdue ones at the right moments (in Now and in conversation, per S25).

## 7 · Reflection & the record (invisible until summoned)

- **S29.** As a user, an immutable ledger of my days and an AI-maintained self-portrait (Identity / Blindspots / Leverage, every claim citing ≥3 dated entries) are built **entirely in the background**. No surface, no panel. I ask for any of it in chat — "show me my blindspots" — and it's there, current, with receipts.
- **S30.** As a user, Friday afternoons the mentor opens the week's impact check **in the regular chat** — what moved per project, what drifted, what to revise. Ten minutes, conversational, no separate ceremony.
- **S31.** As a user, I can see where my attention actually goes per project/domain — zeros included, because the zeros are the message. *(Heat-map visualization — design phase.)* Chosen focus is honored as a named season; unchosen drift gets called, with dates.
- **S32.** Someday: the **Annual Truth** — a once-a-year letter about who I was this year, every claim cited from the ledger.

## 8 · Presence (the Kairos soul)

- **S33.** As a user, the app's resting state is a calm void with rotating quotes I love — and the quote engine is **context-aware**: tuned to the time of day and to what's actually going on in my life (burnout week → gentler company). One engine for all quotes everywhere. I can tap to freeze a quote and it rides into the mentor's context as ambient mood.
- **S34.** As a user, I see today's sunrise and sunset quietly near the date — hidden until hover, or when the moment is near.
- **S35.** As a user, the whole app is Apple-polish calm: keyboard-able (not keyboard-first), undo everywhere with confirms only for the truly important, everything deleted restorable for 30 days, zero data loss ever, restrained dopamine — the payoff is always a plain warm sentence, never confetti.

## 9 · Memory & foundations (promises, not surfaces)

- **S36.** As a user, the mentor remembers durable facts about me, recalls its own memory mid-thought, and I can see, edit, or forget every piece of it in one place.
- **S37.** As a user, my day boundary follows **my local timezone, wherever I am** (no hardcoded 3:30 AM ET).
- **S38.** As a user, MyOS starts fresh — but the mentor can draw on what TRUE learned about me. *(Mechanism: import TRUE's known facts + Life Doc as clearly-labeled seed memory, visible and editable in S36's memory room — the fresh-start feel with none of the amnesia.)*
- **S39.** Under the hood: every input source is one adapter behind one interface (transcripts + reMarkable first; Gmail two-lane, Slack, and multi-account Google Calendar are wired-ready for V2); one learning model; all of TRUE's engineering law (optimistic writes, prompt caching, judgment caching, RLS immutability, injection armor, honest cursors, the decisions log) is day-one standard.

## Locked rulings (July 22, 2026)

**Q1 — Chat-first. No in-app note editor.** The reMarkable IS the notebook; its pages flow in daily through the one door (S10/S26). Kairos's inline-@kai and daily-chapter editor stay out of MyOS. Architecture note: notes remain "just another capture source," so this door stays open forever without owing anything now.

**Q2 — Keep the raw, archived.** Reverses TRUE's burn-the-raw. Everything captured keeps its original alongside the distillate — archived, searchable, re-distillable as models improve. The AI's interpretation is a working copy, never the only source of truth.

**Q3 — No proactivity in V1.** No push, no nudges, no AI-initiated anything. The mentor speaks when spoken to (and in the Friday check, which the user opens by showing up). The forwardness dial (S6) governs conversational energy only. Proactivity is a future conversation, and if it ever comes, the manners spec + bias-to-silence are the law.

**Q4 — One self-knowledge system.** The Ethos folds into the Identity/known-facts layer: principles become a first-class, user-authored section of the one self-portrait (S29/S36). "Fewer, bigger: one big system beats multiple small similar ones."

**Q5 — Design phase.** Arc mode vs per-project color schemes (TRUE's project-as-its-own-colored-world) is the opening question of the UX conversation, not a product ruling.
